# Security Policy

## Overview

Digital Zimbabwe Gold (dZWG) is a Zimbabwe Gold (ZiG; ISO code: ZWG)-denominated digital settlement system designed with strict security, compliance, and governance controls.

Security is a foundational requirement of the dZWG system. This document defines the responsible process for reporting vulnerabilities, security risks, or system integrity concerns related to smart contracts, supporting infrastructure, and associated documentation.

Due to the financial and systemic nature of dZWG, all security matters must be handled through controlled and coordinated disclosure.

---

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you identify a potential security vulnerability, you must not disclose it publicly.

Do not open public GitHub issues or share the vulnerability in any public forum.

All vulnerabilities must be reported directly through the official security contact channel:

**Email:** security@abbaii.com

Reports should include the following:

- a clear and detailed description of the vulnerability  
- steps required to reproduce the issue  
- affected components (contracts, modules, or documentation)  
- potential impact and severity assessment  
- any suggested mitigation or remediation approach (optional)  

Incomplete or vague reports may delay investigation.

---

## Scope

This security policy applies to all components within this repository, including but not limited to:

- smart contract implementations and illustrative contract snippets  
- protocol documentation and system specifications  
- supporting scripts, configurations, and libraries  
- documented compliance and governance frameworks  

It also extends to interactions between on-chain components and described off-chain processes where applicable.

---

## Response Process

All submitted reports will be handled according to the following process:

- acknowledgement of receipt within 72 hours  
- internal triage and severity classification  
- investigation and validation of the reported issue  
- development and testing of mitigation or patch  
- coordinated disclosure once remediation is complete  

Response timelines may vary depending on the complexity and severity of the issue.

---

## Responsible Disclosure Requirements

All reporters are expected to adhere to responsible disclosure practices.

Specifically:

- vulnerabilities must not be exploited under any circumstances  
- vulnerabilities must not be disclosed publicly before remediation  
- no attempt should be made to access, modify, or disrupt system data or operations  
- all findings must be communicated exclusively through the official security channel  

Failure to adhere to these requirements may result in disqualification of the report and potential legal or regulatory consequences.

---

## System Integrity and Governance

The dZWG system operates under a controlled governance model with restricted access to core components.

Security controls include, but are not limited to:

- role-based access restrictions  
- multisignature administrative controls  
- timelocked upgrade mechanisms  
- controlled deployment and release processes  

These controls are designed to minimize attack surfaces and ensure operational integrity across the system.

---

## Contact

All security-related communication must be directed to:

**Email:** security@abbaii.com

No other communication channels are authorized for vulnerability reporting.

## Notice

This repository forms part of a regulated and controlled financial infrastructure system.

Security disclosures are treated with strict confidentiality and handled under structured internal processes to ensure system stability, user protection, and regulatory alignment.
